This year, I will be talking as part of the Atoms to Galaxies session alongside interesting talks being given by Dr. Susan Cartwright and.... As a taster, here is the title and abstract for my upcoming talk.

Title: 1,000,000 degrees with a chance of solar flares
Abstract: Our Sun displays some of the most dynamic and extreme events in the solar system. In this talk, I will present a weather report of interesting solar weather phenomena e.g. solar wind, coronal rain, and the immense heat of the solar atmosphere that defies explanation after decades of research. We will look back through history at how the largest solar weather events have left their mark on the Earth. We will aim to understand why such violent weather phenomena occur and how they can have catastrophic consequences on Earth. Are we overdue the next major solar event? Are we prepared?

I hope you can come along to enjoy an evening of open discussion about some of the most interesting parts of scientific research in Sheffield.
